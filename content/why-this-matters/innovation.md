R&D spending as a share of GDP is a leading indicator of future productivity and export competitiveness — the innovation gauge is upstream of the productivity gauge elsewhere on this Scorecard, not a duplicate of it. Australia has historically spent less on R&D relative to GDP than many high-income peers, and successive government reviews (including the 2024 Strategic Examination of R&D) have flagged this as a structural weakness. A country that under-invests in R&D today is choosing slower productivity growth a decade from now.
