This gauge is the flip side of Education elsewhere on this Scorecard: PISA measures how well school-leavers are performing today, while tertiary attainment among 25-34 year-olds measures how many of them go on to complete a university or equivalent qualification — the stock of highly-skilled young workers actually entering the workforce. A country can have strong school results but a lower tertiary completion rate (or vice versa) depending on its post-school education system and labour market, so the two gauges are deliberately kept separate rather than merged into one.
