A country's trajectory isn't just economic — it also depends on whether people trust each other and their institutions enough to pull in the same direction when times get hard. This gauge tracks how genuinely civil society organisations participate in political life: whether they're routinely consulted, whether ordinary people are actually involved rather than nominally represented. It's an imperfect proxy for social cohesion (no single number fully captures it), but a participatory civil society is one of the more measurable, comparable signals available.
