Productivity — output per hour worked — is the long-run engine behind rising living standards. Countries can grow their economies for a while just by adding more workers or more hours, but that runs out; sustained wage growth ultimately requires each hour of work producing more value. Australia's productivity growth has been a persistent policy worry for over a decade, cited by the Productivity Commission and RBA alike. This gauge tracks whether that gap against top performers is closing or widening, independent of population growth or the mining-investment cycle.
