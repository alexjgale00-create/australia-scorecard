The price-to-income ratio is a more honest measure of housing affordability than the raw house price alone, since it accounts for whether wages have kept pace. Australia — alongside Canada and New Zealand in this comparison set — has become a byword internationally for housing unaffordability, with capital city prices repeatedly outpacing income growth for over a decade. This gauge connects directly to Debt burden elsewhere on this Scorecard: expensive housing, financed by large mortgages, is the main driver of Australia's unusually high household debt.
