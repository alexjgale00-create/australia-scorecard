The Gini coefficient measures how evenly disposable income is spread across the population — a persistently high or rising figure means growth isn't reaching most households even when headline GDP looks healthy. It's a companion to living-standards: a country can post strong average income growth while most of the gain concentrates at the top, and this gauge is what catches that. Wealth inequality (a related but distinct measure) is shown for context on this gauge's detail page but isn't part of its score — see the Methodology page for why.
