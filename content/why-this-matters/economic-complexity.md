Economic complexity measures how diversified and knowledge-intensive a country's exports are — not how much it exports, but how hard what it exports would be for another country to replicate. It matters because commodity-heavy export baskets (Australia's iron ore and coal, historically) are vulnerable to price swings and don't build the same depth of institutional knowhow as complex manufacturing or services. This gauge is a check on whether Australia's economy is diversifying beyond resources over time, independent of the commodity price cycle.
