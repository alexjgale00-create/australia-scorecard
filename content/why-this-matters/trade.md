Australia's share of world exports is a measure of economic weight, not just economic health — a country can grow strongly at home while its share of global trade shrinks simply because other economies are growing and trading faster. Australia's exports are heavily concentrated in a handful of commodities (iron ore, coal, natural gas), which makes this gauge unusually sensitive to global commodity price cycles rather than just underlying competitiveness. Watch this one alongside the "why this matters" note on External position — the two are closely linked.
