Military spending is a proxy for a country's willingness and ability to back its strategic commitments — alliance credibility, deterrence, and capacity to respond to a crisis without relying entirely on partners. It's a deliberately narrow lens: it says nothing about how wisely that money is spent, or whether more spending actually buys more security. Read alongside external-position and trade, it's one piece of how exposed or self-reliant Australia is in a less stable region.
