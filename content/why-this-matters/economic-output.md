This gauge answers a different question from Living standards: not "how well-off is the average Australian" but "how much does Australia's economy weigh in the world, full stop." A country's global share of GDP shapes its bargaining power in trade negotiations, its voice in forums like the G20, and how much its domestic policy choices ripple internationally. Australia's share has been drifting down for years — not because the Australian economy is shrinking, but because China, India, and other large emerging economies are growing faster than the world average. A falling share here is compatible with rising living standards at home; they're measuring different things.
